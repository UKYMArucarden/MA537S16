ASSIGNMENTS:

Three to four problems will be assigned each week. You may collaborate on the problems, but your write-up and your programs must be your own independent work. LATE POLICY:

An assignment is considered late, if it is not turned in either in class or through Canvas on or before the due date. You may turn in two problem sets one class period late without penalty. The instructor reserves the right to penalize all subsequent late assignments at most 20% each. Keep in mind that solutions to assignments will be posted prior to exams, and once the solution to an assignment has been posted that assignment can longer be submitted as a late assignment.

EXAMS:

Three timed, closed-book exams will together account for around 20% of the final grade. The first will take place in mid-February, the second in Late March, and the third during the final exam period. At least two of the three exams will take place in class.

ACADEMIC INTEGRITY:

Violations of academic integrity will be taken seriously and dealt with according to university regulations. Instances of cheating on exams include (but are not limited to) copying from or communicating with another student, bringing any kind of notes into the exam unless expressly permitted, and using any type of electronic aid unless expressly permitted. You are encouraged to work together on homework, but you are required to write up and submit your own solutions to all assignments. This includes computer codes: You may consult with other students about programs but must write your own code. Students caught cheating will be prosecuted according to university guidelines.
